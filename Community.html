Professionals and Volunteers

The team

A very important part of our community are the following three therapists: Gillian Stokes, Carmel Hartley and Kiera Appleton. These are professionals who have assisted students before and have agreed to work with us. They are kind and caring people and we encourage you to reach out to them if needed. We also recommend checking out their websites.

Gillian Stokes

I have had 8 years of experience practicing therapy with young adults. My approach is rooted in Biofeedback and Basement Therapy. Whether you're dealing with Panic, Phobia, or Geniuses, I am honored to pull up a chair and listen to your story. My therapy style is conversational and authentic. As a therapist, I seek to help you ***be your best*** self. I will meet you where you are on your journey. I believe you ARE worth it!!!.

Carmel Hartley

I have had 8 years of experience practicing therapy with teens. My approach is rooted in Integrative Therapy and Predictability Therapy. Whether you're dealing with Emotion Regulation, Epigenetics, or Sleuths, we can work together. My therapy style is grounded and directive. As a therapist, I seek to help you re-discover your inherent strengths and skills. I will consider it an honor and a privilige to walk this journey with you. I believe establishing a strong therapeutic relationship is one of the most important foundations in the healing process.

Kiera Appleton

I have extensive experience working with adolescents. My approach is rooted in Social Recovery Therapy and Registered Mink Therapy. Whether you're dealing with Friends, Pediatric Autoimmune Neuropsychiatric, or Batteries, you deserve a counselor who understands you. My therapy style is authentic and conversational. As a therapist, I work hard to create the sense of safety by talking 'with' you and not 'at' you. I will never judge; I understand. I believe everyone heals differently.

Our groups

Below you will find a list of groups. Joining and talking to the members is completely free. Every group focuses on a specific problem you may have. All members of each group are either volunteers who have agreed to work with us or fellow students who have gone through these problems. Everyone is kind and understanding. There is no need to be scared. Remember they just want to help you, not judge you.

Dealing with burnout

Burnout can happen to anyone and it’s ok. Talk to people who have gone through it and learn about their experience and how they dealt with it.

Family problems

We can’t always control what happens in our family, but that doesn’t mean we are immune to it. Talk to people who can help you go through college regardless of family stress.

College and disability

Going to college with any kind of disability isn't easy, but it can be done. Meet people with disabilities and learn about their experience. Remember your disability doesn’t define you.

Dealing with stress

College is stressful for a lot of people, but it doesn’t have to be. Talk to people who can help you deal with this stress and give you advice based on their experience.

Financial problems

Our financial situation can cause a lot of stress. If you are struggling to get by, talk to people who are well informed about scholarships and financial aid for students. We want to help.

College and mental illness

Our mental health affects our daily life. There is no need to be ashamed because of it. Talk to people who have had similar experiences. You will get through this.
